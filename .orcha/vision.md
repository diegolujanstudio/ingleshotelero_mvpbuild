# Vision — Inglés Hotelero

## The problem in one sentence
Every hotel in Latin America with international guests has staff who freeze, fumble, or fail when a guest speaks English — and every existing solution (in-person trainers, generic apps, classroom courses) has failed to solve it at scale.

## Why we win
- **Relevance.** Content is built from real hotel scenarios, not textbooks. A finite vocabulary of 200–400 functional phrases per role is learnable in 3 months, not "study forever."
- **Access.** 24/7 on the employee's own phone via web app and WhatsApp. No classroom, no schedule.
- **Measurement.** HR sees exactly who is practicing, who is improving, who needs help. Training becomes an auditable investment, not a cost center.
- **Affordability.** Costs less than a single instructor and covers every shift, every department, simultaneously.
- **Speed.** An employee is evaluated in 15 minutes. A hotel can assess its entire team in a week.

## What we become
1. **Phase 1 (now):** Placement exam + training for hotel English. The wedge.
2. **Phase 2 (6–12 mo):** Workforce development platform for hospitality — English + soft skills, complaint handling, upselling.
3. **Phase 3 (12–24 mo):** Talent intelligence layer for LatAm hospitality — bilingual certification hotels advertise. The moat.

## Business model
- **Land:** $50 USD/employee placement exam (one-time).
- **Expand:** $150–$500/month/property training subscription.
- **Lock-in:** "Propiedad Certificada en Servicio Bilingüe" program.

## Core pedagogical principle
Hotel English is a **finite skill**, not an academic subject. A front desk agent needs ~300–400 functional phrases to handle 95% of guest interactions professionally. The system teaches the English the job requires — not English in general. Success is measured by functional performance ("Can you handle a check-in complaint?"), not academic scores.

## Daily learning loop (5 min/day)
1. **Listen (1 min)** — hear a guest say something in English, select the correct action.
2. **Speak (2 min)** — read a scenario in Spanish, respond in English via voice note, AI scores it.
3. **Reinforce (1 min)** — see + hear the model response, re-record.
4. **Review (1 min)** — 3 vocabulary flashcards via spaced repetition.

## Level progression per role module
A1 (survival) → A2 (functional) → B1 (professional) → B2 (advanced). Each module is 3 months. Modules are per role: bellboy, front desk, restaurant/bar.
